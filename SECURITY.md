# Security and Responsible Disclosure Policy

We take the security of this project and its users seriously. We want security
researchers to feel comfortable reporting vulnerabilities they have discovered so
we can fix them and keep users safe. This policy reflects our values and our
commitment to working in good faith with researchers who share their expertise
with us.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Please report suspected security vulnerabilities privately to the maintainers
rather than opening a public issue. You can reach us at `opensource@undp.org`, or
contact a maintainer directly (see [COMMUNITY.md](COMMUNITY.md)). If your platform
supports it, you may also use GitHub's private security advisory feature
("Report a vulnerability" under the repository's Security tab).

Reports may be submitted anonymously. If you share contact information, we will
acknowledge receipt of your report within 3 business days.

When reporting, please include enough detail for us to reproduce and assess the
issue: a description of the vulnerability, the affected component or version, and
step-by-step reproduction instructions or proof-of-concept where possible.

## Coordinated disclosure

We ask that you give us a reasonable opportunity to investigate and remediate a
reported issue before disclosing it publicly. Unless otherwise agreed, please wait
**at least 90 days** from your initial report (or until a fix has been released,
whichever comes first) before publicly disclosing details. We will keep you
informed of our progress and will credit reporters who wish to be acknowledged.

## Scope and good-faith research

This policy applies to the source code in this repository. Please avoid actions
that could harm users or data, such as accessing data that is not your own,
degrading service availability, or running automated scans that disrupt others.
Research conducted in good faith and consistent with this policy is welcome.

## Privacy

Any personal data handled by this project, and any personal data you share when
reporting an issue, is processed in accordance with our privacy policy (see
`PRIVACY.md` where present). We will not share reporter contact details beyond
what is necessary to triage and resolve the report.
